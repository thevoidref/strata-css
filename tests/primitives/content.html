Paragraphs

Paragraph variants

Lead text introduces a page or major section. It gives the opening message greater presence and establishes context for the content that follows.

Strong text gives important supporting content greater emphasis. It is useful for prominent statements, summaries, and key information that should stand out.

Primary text is the default for body copy. It is used for ordinary reading, explanations, descriptions, and general content.

Muted text carries low-priority supporting information. It is suited to notes, metadata, contextual details, and information that should remain unobtrusive.

Disabled text represents unavailable or inactive content. It should be reserved for interface states rather than used as a general level of text emphasis.
